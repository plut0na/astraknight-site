Privacy Policy — Mind Trace

Effective date: 6 June 2026

Last updated: 21 July 2026

Developer: Astraknight

Contact: team@astraknight.com

This policy explains what information Mind Trace ("the app", "we", "us") collects from you when you use it, how that information is used, and the controls you have over it.

We have written this policy in plain English. We are committed to collecting the minimum amount of information necessary to run the app and keep it working, and to never selling your personal data. Mind Trace is an independent experiment — the analytics described below exist to help us understand whether the game finds an audience and how players engage with it, not to build a profile of you as an individual.

Summary

  • Mind Trace does not require an account, login, or any personal information from you to play.
  • Mind Trace does not access your contacts, photos, location (GPS), microphone, camera, files, or any other sensitive device data.
  • The app has Google AdMob (advertising) and Google Firebase (anonymous analytics and crash reporting) integrated. Advertising is currently switched off — see Section 2 for what would apply if it's enabled in future. Firebase's anonymous analytics and crash reporting operate regardless.
  • All data flowing to those services is anonymous — we cannot tie any analytics event or crash report to a real-world person.
  • Mind Trace is not directed at children under 13.

Information we collect, and why

1. Information stored only on your device (not transmitted)

Mind Trace stores the following on your device using your operating system's standard preferences storage. None of this leaves your device. It is not "collected" in the regulatory sense; we cannot see or access it.

  • Your highest level reached
  • Your Memory Rating score
  • Your sound, music, and haptic preferences
  • Run counters used to space out app-improvement analytics and (if enabled) ad placements

When you uninstall the app, all of this data is removed from your device by your operating system.

2. Information sent to Google AdMob (advertising) — currently inactive

Mind Trace has Google's AdMob SDK integrated, capable of showing a rewarded ad you can choose to watch to continue after running out of lives, and occasional interstitial ads between runs. Advertising is currently switched off — no ads are shown, and none of the data described in this section is currently collected. Astraknight may enable advertising at any point at its own discretion; if and when that happens, this policy's "Last updated" date will change and, while active, AdMob would collect:

  • Advertising identifier (Google Advertising ID). A randomised, reset-able ID managed by your operating system, not your name or email.
  • Device information — device model, operating system version, language, and country/region derived from your IP address.
  • App context — that Mind Trace was the app where the ad was shown, and at what point.

AdMob would use this information to choose which ad to show you and to report aggregate ad performance to advertisers. AdMob is operated by Google. Their handling of this data is governed by Google's privacy policy.

You can reset your advertising ID, or opt out of personalised advertising, at any time via your device's system settings (Android: Settings → Google → Ads) — this takes effect immediately should advertising ever be enabled.

3. Information sent to Google Firebase (analytics + stability)

To understand how players use Mind Trace and to find and fix crashes, the app uses two Firebase services:

Firebase Analytics collects anonymous information about gameplay events such as:

  • When a level starts, completes, or fails
  • When a run ends, when a revive ad is watched, when an interstitial is shown
  • Session duration, app version, device model, operating system
  • Approximate location (country / region) derived from your IP address
  • Your advertising identifier (the same one used by AdMob)

Firebase Crashlytics collects anonymous information about app crashes:

  • The technical stack trace of the crash
  • Device model, operating system version, app version
  • The state Mind Trace was in when the crash occurred (current level, run count, etc.)

Both services are operated by Google. Their handling of this data is governed by Google's privacy policy.

We use this information to understand whether Mind Trace is finding an audience and how players engage with and return to it — the questions at the core of this experiment — and to find and fix crashes. We do not use it for advertising, profiling, or any commercial purpose other than running Mind Trace.

4. Information we explicitly do NOT collect

  • Your name, email, phone number, or any other contact information
  • Your photos, files, contacts, calendar, microphone, or camera input
  • Your precise (GPS) location
  • Your social media accounts or identities from other apps
  • Any account, password, or payment information (Mind Trace has no accounts and no in-app purchases)

Sharing

We do not sell your data. We do not share it with marketers, brokers, or any third party other than the Google services described above. Google's services receive the data flows described in sections 2 and 3 strictly to provide the advertising, analytics, and crash-reporting functions Mind Trace uses.

Your controls

Consent (EU / EEA / UK)

If your device's region is set to the EU, EEA, or UK, Mind Trace shows you a consent dialog the first time you open it, asking about your choices around data processing. This dialog covers two things, one dormant and one active today:

  • Personalised advertising — dormant. Advertising is currently switched off (see Section 2), so this part of your choice has no effect yet. If and when advertising is enabled, accepting lets AdMob use your advertising ID to choose more relevant ads; declining limits it to non-personalised ads. The app works normally either way.
  • Firebase Analytics and Crashlytics collection — active today. If you choose the dialog's strictest option (declining all data processing, not just ad personalisation), Firebase Analytics and Crashlytics stop collecting data from your device immediately. Choosing to allow non-personalised processing keeps them active, even while advertising itself remains off.

You can change your consent choice at any time from the in-app settings ("Manage privacy").

Advertising identifier reset / opt-out

Your operating system gives you the ability to reset your advertising identifier or opt out of personalised advertising at any time:

  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → "Delete advertising ID" or "Opt out of Ads Personalization".

These controls apply across all apps on your device, not just Mind Trace.

Deletion

Mind Trace does not maintain a server-side record of you, so there is no account to delete. The data stored on your device (your progress, settings, etc.) is removed by your operating system when you uninstall the app. The anonymous analytics events Firebase has already received cannot be tied back to you individually, but you can request Google delete data associated with your advertising identifier via Google's privacy controls at myadcenter.google.com.

Children

Mind Trace is not directed at children under 13.

We do not knowingly collect any information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and you believe a child under 13 has used Mind Trace, please contact us at the email at the top of this policy and we will take reasonable steps to address the situation.

This app is rated for all ages but, because of the advertising component, it is not part of Google Play's Designed for Families program.

International users

Mind Trace is operated from the United Kingdom. Firebase Analytics and Google AdMob, the third-party services Mind Trace uses, operate globally. If you access Mind Trace from outside the United Kingdom, please note that data may be transferred to and processed in countries with data protection laws different from your own — specifically the United States, where Google's servers are located.

Google participates in the EU-US Data Privacy Framework and equivalent mechanisms covering the UK and Switzerland; see Google's privacy policy for details.

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, the CCPA gives you specific rights regarding your personal information.

Personal information collected: the information sent to AdMob and Firebase as described in sections 2 and 3 above. The categories covered are: identifiers (advertising ID), internet activity (app interactions logged by Firebase Analytics), and geolocation data (country-level only, derived from IP).

Sources: all such information comes directly from your use of the app and your device.

Purposes: to understand app usage and demand, to diagnose crashes, and — only if and when advertising is enabled (see Section 2; it currently isn't) — to serve ads.

Sale of personal information: we do not sell your personal information. If advertising is ever enabled, we would share advertising identifiers with Google AdMob for the limited purpose of serving ads; while advertising is switched off, as it currently is, no such sharing occurs.

Your rights: you can request information about what we hold (it is the categories listed above), and you can request deletion (limited by the same anonymity constraint described in "Your controls → Deletion").

To exercise these rights, contact us at the email at the top of this policy.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy occasionally — for example, if we add a new third-party service or remove one. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy. If the change is material (e.g. we begin collecting a new category of data), we will notify users in-app the next time they open Mind Trace.
