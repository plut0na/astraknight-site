Mind Trace does not require an account, login, or any personal information to play, and it does not store any data about you on our own servers. This page explains exactly what data exists, how to delete it, and how to ask us for help.

What data exists

  • On your device only. Your progress, scores, and settings are saved locally on your device. We cannot see or access this data, and it never reaches us.
  • Anonymous data held by Google. Mind Trace uses Google AdMob (ads) and Google Firebase (anonymous analytics and crash reporting). These collect technical data — such as your device's resettable advertising identifier, device model, and country (from your IP) — that is not linked to your name or any account. Full detail is in our Privacy Policy.

How to delete your data

  1. Delete the on-device data. Uninstalling Mind Trace immediately removes all locally stored progress and settings from your device.
  2. Reset or delete your advertising identifier. On Android: Settings → Google → Ads, then choose "Delete advertising ID" or "Opt out of Ads Personalization". This disconnects future ad and analytics data from your device.
  3. Ask Google to delete data tied to your advertising ID. Because the data Google holds is keyed to that identifier, you can request its deletion through Google's own controls at myadcenter.google.com and myactivity.google.com.
  4. Contact us. Email team@astraknight.com with the subject "Mind Trace data deletion request" and we will take any further reasonable steps available to us. We aim to respond within 14 days.

An important note on anonymous data

Because Mind Trace collects no accounts and no personal identifiers, the analytics and ad data is anonymous and cannot be traced back to an individual person. This means there is no personal record on our side to look up or remove on request — but the steps above let you delete the on-device data yourself and sever and remove the identifier-linked data held by Google.

Questions about this process? Email team@astraknight.com.
